Understanding Ticket Types for Amsterdam’s Public Transport

As a visitor, here are the main ticket options available for using public transport in Amsterdam. Choose the best option based on your needs:

1. OV-chipkaart

The OV-chipkaart is a smart card that works across all public transport modes (trams, buses, and metros). You can either buy an anonymous OV-chipkaart or opt for a disposable paper version for short-term stays.

  • Where to Buy: Ticket machines at stations, supermarkets, and kiosks.
  • Cost: €7.50 for the card itself (excludes travel credit).
  • How to Use: Tap the card on readers at stations or onboard to check in and out.
More information here

2. Day Passes (1–7 Days)

Day passes allow unlimited travel on all GVB services for a set number of days. Ideal for tourists who want to explore the city without worrying about purchasing multiple tickets.

  • Duration: Available from 1 to 7 days.
  • Cost: From €8.50 for 1 day, up to €37 for 7 days.
  • Where to Buy: Purchase at GVB machines, kiosks, or through the GVB app.
More information here

3. Single Tickets

If you’re only traveling within the city for a short time, single-use tickets are a good option.

  • Duration: Valid for 1 hour after activation.
  • Cost: €3.40 (subject to change).
  • Where to Buy: Available at GVB ticket machines, onboard trams and buses, or via mobile apps.
More information here

4. Amsterdam City Card

The I Amsterdam City Card offers a combination of unlimited public transport and free or discounted access to many museums and attractions.

  • Duration: Available in 24, 48, 72, 96, and 120-hour options.
  • Cost: Starting at €65 (subject to change).
  • Where to Buy: Available online, at visitor centers, or selected hotels.
